What is MoonUp?

We're are a small group of astronomers based in Cork city, with a particular interest in the lunar landscape. From harvest moon to blood moon, crescent to quarter to full, the moon remains a familiar presence in the sky, almost something we take for granted. We want to share our knowledge and give people a detailed view of our moon, a view they may not have experienced before. When the moon's up, we're out. We organise regular Moon-watches so check back regularly or sign-up to our newsletter to keep up-to-date.

Where are you?

For moon-watches we usually observe in Kennedy Park, near Cork city center as the moon's luminosity is such that it's not overpowered by the city lights.


We also go beyond the moon from time to time. We use Farran Woods as an observing site for fainter objects. Even though it's only a 20min drive from the city center it provides much darker skies. We've viewed the International Space Station (ISS), meteor showers or more distant planets at this location. Find out more here. Please tell your friends and family, the more the merrier. We try to car-pool as much as possible. Dogs welcome!

Any tips?

We advise you to dress warmly. If possible bring a hot drink and some snacks. A collapsible camping chair will be useful. Bring binoculars if you can, they provide good views of the moon and allow you to keep observing when waiting for your turn on the scopes. Bring a DSLR if you have one and we can show you how to start astro-photography.
